# Stratum Demo Assets

Canonical source: [docs/gtm/stratum-gtm-canonical.md](./stratum-gtm-canonical.md)

## 60-Second Recording Script

1. Screen: home page with the company search box.
   Click: enter a target company and click `Build brief`.
   Say: "Stratum starts with a point-in-time brief from supported ATS sources."
   Pause: when the brief loads.
2. Screen: saved brief summary and proof roles.
   Click: none.
   Say: "This is the evidence-backed brief, with source coverage, proof roles, and caveats."
   Pause: on the proof roles and coverage section.
3. Screen: open the watchlist or entry detail.
   Click: `Open watchlists` or `Open Entry Detail`.
   Say: "Stratum keeps the latest saved brief separate from the previous one, so the comparison stays explicit."
   Pause: on the latest vs previous comparison.
4. Screen: scheduled refresh and inbox.
   Click: open the schedule and then the inbox.
   Say: "You can run this manually or on a schedule, and meaningful changes land in the in-app inbox."
   Pause: on the inbox list.
5. Screen: limits section.
   Click: none.
   Say: "The limit is important: this is supported ATS coverage for a finite list, not full-company intelligence, and not an external alerting layer."
   Pause: after the limits line.

## 2-Minute Recording Script

1. Screen: home page.
   Click: enter a target company and build the brief.
   Say: "Stratum is built for corp dev and investor teams that track a small list of target companies. The goal is not to summarize the whole company. The goal is to save a clean point-in-time read and compare it to the next one."
   Pause: after the brief appears.
2. Screen: brief overview.
   Click: none.
   Say: "Here you can see the matched company, the observed roles, the source coverage, and the caveats. That makes the brief useful as a working artifact instead of a loose search result."
   Pause: on proof roles and coverage.
3. Screen: watchlist detail.
   Click: open the watchlist and the tracked entry.
   Say: "Each tracked company keeps a saved-brief history. Stratum compares the latest saved brief to the previous saved brief, so the change record stays readable over time."
   Pause: on latest vs previous.
4. Screen: schedule controls.
   Click: set or review the schedule.
   Say: "Monitoring can run manually or on a schedule. The important part is that refresh attempts stay separate from saved briefs, so you can trust the record."
   Pause: on the schedule status.
5. Screen: inbox.
   Click: open the inbox.
   Say: "Meaningful changes surface here, inside the product. That keeps the workflow simple: review, mark read, dismiss, or jump back to the entry."
   Pause: on an unread item.
6. Screen: limits section.
   Click: none.
   Say: "The honest limit is that Stratum only covers supported ATS sources for the companies you choose. It does not claim full-company intelligence, and it does not send external alerts yet."
   Pause: after the limit line.
